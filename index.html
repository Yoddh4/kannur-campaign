The Manifesto: Action Over Assurances

I am committed to actively advocating for immediate administrative relief regarding our pressing logistical hurdles and protecting the academic standards of our discipline.

1. Fixing FYUGP & Internships

The transition to the Four-Year Undergraduate Programme (FYUGP) has brought immense workload confusion and erratic course layouts.

The Plan: Demand a streamlined, predictable academic calendar. For the mandatory 2-credit internships, I will advocate for a centralized directory of host institutions and an evaluation mechanism that does not exploit our valuable time.

2. Reforming the K-REAP Portal

Continuous technical glitches, complex data-entry, and non-intuitive workflows have literally transformed our teaching hours into clerical desk jobs.

The Plan: Force the university to interface with MKCL/developers to simplify the portal and remove redundant entries. If unresolved, push to replace it with successful in-house models like the Digital University of Kerala portal.

3. Upgrading to PG Centers

While our UG student intake is rising toward 700 seats, very few of our 15 departments have been upgraded to postgraduate centers.

The Plan: Strongly advocate for sanctioning new MA History streams in colleges demonstrating consistent UG stability. This will create vital avenues for PG teaching, dissertation guidance, and faculty research growth.

4. Addressing Grade Compression

With extreme compression into B and C grades, our graduates face intense competition securing B.Ed. admissions compared to subjects like Economics.

The Plan: Protect our academic standards while pushing for centralized resource-pooling and standardized question banks to uplift vulnerable departments dropping to 35% pass rates.
